## Key Points (5–10 bullets)
- F-1 students may work on campus at Illinois Tech while enrolled full-time and maintaining valid F-1 status.
- On-campus employment is limited to **20 hours/week or less during academic terms** and **full-time during official school breaks** (with stated exceptions).
- The **I-20 serves as proof of F-1 on-campus work authorization** (for Illinois Tech on-campus jobs).
- Students typically need an **SSN**; they may start with an **SSN receipt**, but must provide the physical SSN card within **4 weeks**.
- On-campus jobs applications are accepted **only through Handshake** (as stated).
- J-1 students require **separate OGS authorization before starting** any on-campus employment.
- Students enrolled less than full-time (e.g., last semester) require **special OGS approval** before working.
- Changes (hours/employer/location/termination) must be reported; unauthorized work can jeopardize immigration status.

## Rules / Requirements
- Maintain valid immigration status (F-1 or J-1) and good academic standing.
- Be enrolled full-time (UG: 12 credits; Grad: 9 credits) unless an approved exception applies.
- Must not exceed **20 hours/week or less during academic sessions**.
- Full-time is allowed during official school vacation periods **except** when summer is the **first or last term** (then the 20 hours/week limit applies).
- J-1 students must obtain OGS work authorization **before** beginning employment.
- SSN documentation must be provided to the Student Employment Office within **4 weeks** of starting work (receipt allowed initially; physical SSN card required).

## Clean Policy Text

### Overview
International students may work in eligible on-campus positions at Illinois Institute of Technology with proper authorization. On-campus employment includes jobs paid by Illinois Tech and certain on-site commercial services that directly support students. Students must confirm eligibility and authorization with the Office of Global Services (OGS) before working.

### Eligibility

#### F-1 Students
F-1 students may work on campus at the institution that issued their I-20 while enrolled in a full course of study. The I-20 serves as proof of employment eligibility. To remain eligible, students must:
- Maintain full-time enrollment (UG: 12 credits; Grad: 9 credits)
- Be in good academic standing
- Hold a valid, unexpired I-20

##### Exceptions (Final Semester / Reduced Enrollment)
Students in their final semester who are enrolled less than full-time must obtain special permission from OGS to work on campus. Once approved:
- A **paper Payroll Authorization (PA)** must be submitted (EPAF will be rejected)
- The OGS approval email and paper PA must be sent to the Student Employment Office contact provided by Illinois Tech

#### J-1 Students
J-1 students may work on campus only with **prior authorization from OGS**. Eligible employment includes:
- Work required by a scholarship or assistantship (teaching/research)
- On-campus employment at Illinois Tech
- Off-campus employment due to serious, urgent, or unforeseen economic circumstances (with authorization)

J-1 students must submit an **“On-Campus Work Authorization” request** in the ISSS Portal before beginning work. Sponsored J-1 students (e.g., Fulbright) must also obtain approval from their sponsoring agency.

### Work Hours (Limits)
- Maximum of **20 hours/week or less during academic terms**
- Full-time employment allowed during official school vacation periods
- If summer is the **first or last term**, students may work **only up to 20 hours/week**
- Any change in hours, employer, job type, location, or early termination must be reported and may require new authorization

### Application and Hiring Process (as stated)

#### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility
- Review employment eligibility rules on the OGS website
- Consult the Illinois Tech Student Employment Handbook if needed

#### Step 2: Apply to Jobs
- Apply **only through Handshake**
- Do not apply in person or via email
- Resume and cover letter support is available through Career Services

#### Step 3: Payroll Authorization (PA)
- After accepting a job, the supervisor submits a **Payroll Authorization (PA)**
- Most students use the **Electronic Payroll Authorization Form (EPAF)**
- Some roles (e.g., Graduate/Teaching/Research Assistants) or exceptions may require a paper PA

#### Step 4: Job Offer Letter for SSN
- The hiring manager submits an **SSA Offer Letter request** after EPAF submission
- The job offer letter is generated and signed (supervisor + OGS) and emailed to the student
- This letter is required to apply for a Social Security Number (SSN)

#### Step 5: Obtain a Social Security Number (SSN)
- Students apply for an SSN at the Social Security Administration using the job offer letter and required documents
- Students receive a receipt immediately; the physical SSN card arrives in ~2–4 weeks
- Students may work for up to **4 weeks** with the SSN receipt

#### Step 6: Submit Hiring Documents
- Submit the SSN receipt and all required hiring paperwork to the Student Employment Office
- Complete required training, timesheet setup, and onboarding steps

#### Step 7: Present SSN Within Four Weeks
- Present the physical SSN card to the Student Employment Office within **4 weeks** of application
- Failure to do so may result in **employment termination**
- Departments may be notified if compliance deadlines are not met

### Reporting Changes and Compliance
- Any change in employment (hours, employer, location, type of job, or early completion) must be reported
- J-1 students must submit a new authorization request for such changes
- Unauthorized employment is a violation of immigration status and may have serious consequences

### Important Notes
- OGS approval does **not** replace Student Employment Office procedures
- Students are not eligible to work until all required authorizations are in place
- Consult OGS for clarification regarding visa status and employment eligibility
