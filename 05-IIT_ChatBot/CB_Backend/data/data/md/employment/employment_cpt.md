## Clean Policy Text

### Overview
Curricular Practical Training (CPT) is temporary employment that allows an F-1 student to gain practical experience through internships, cooperative education, or practicums. It is an integral part of the curriculum and must be directly related to the student’s major field of study. CPT is either a required part of the curriculum or taken for credit.

### Eligibility
- Must have been in lawful full-time F-1 status for at least one academic year in a degree-seeking program.
- Employment offer must be directly related to the major field of study as listed on the I-20.

### Academic Standing
- Undergraduates: 2.25 GPA minimum.
- Graduate students: 3.0 GPA minimum.
- Some departments may require a higher GPA.
- Students on academic probation cannot pursue/continue co-op/internship programs.

### Approvals and Authorization
To apply for CPT, students work with their academic department, Career Services, or the Career Management Center for initial approvals. Certain programs (Chicago-Kent College of Law, Institute of Design, Psychology) coordinate with academic units and OGS. Final approval is an updated I-20 issued by OGS.

### Authorization Period and Work Hours
Work authorization is granted by academic terms (fall, spring, summer); each term requires a new application or renewal. If approved, the I-20 lists employer information, start date, end date, and authorization for:
- Part-time: 20 hours/week or less
- Full-time: more than 20 hours/week

### Enrollment Requirements
- If enrolled in an internship during fall/spring, students must be enrolled in 12 undergraduate or nine graduate credits of coursework/research in addition to internship credits.
- If enrolling during the last term, students must also be enrolled in at least one live course.

### Application Steps (as stated)
1. Work with academic department/Career Services/Career Management Center for initial approvals (program-specific exceptions apply).
2. Submit a “Curricular Practical Training (CPT)” request in the ISSS Portal and upload the required CPT Form.
3. Be properly registered for the internship course associated with the CPT application.
4. OGS processing time is estimated at 7 business days (no expedited processing).
5. Receive the new CPT I-20 from OGS and begin working only from the approved start date on the I-20.

### Loss or Changes in Employment
If employment is terminated or details change, notify Experiential Learning (explearning@illinoistech.edu) and OGS (global@illinoistech.edu) within 10 days. OGS cannot backdate authorized CPT dates once the authorization period has passed.
