## Rules / Requirements
- Form I-983 must be fully completed (no blank/incorrect sections) or OGS will require resubmission.
- Typed responses are preferred; handwriting must be legible.
- SEVIS school code must match the student’s campus I-20.
- DSO name/contact must match the OGS official listed on the most recent I-20.
- STEM OPT requested period must align: starts right after OPT ends and lasts exactly 24 months.

## Clean Policy Text

### Overview / Completeness Requirements
Form I-983 must be filled in completely and correctly. If any box or section is left blank or incorrect, OGS will not be able to process the request and will ask the student to resubmit the form with updates, which can delay processing time.

Whenever possible, responses on Form I-983 should be typed. If handwritten, all handwriting must be legible; otherwise, OGS will request resubmission.

If assistance is needed, students should refer to official DHS instructions:
- Form I-983 written instructions (linked below)

---

### Section 1: Student Information

#### SEVIS School Code (School Recommending STEM OPT)
The SEVIS school code is found in the School Information section on page one of the new format I-20 and depends on the campus:
- Mies (Main) Campus: CHI214F00379000
- Conviser Law Center: CHI214F00379003
- Institute of Design: CHI214F00379002

#### DSO Name and Contact Information
Use the OGS member listed on the most recently issued I-20 (Page 1 – School Information section).

#### STEM OPT Requested Period
For the 24-month extension, the requested period begins immediately after the initial OPT expires and ends exactly 24 months later.

Example:
- OPT expires: May 31, 2016
- STEM OPT requested period: June 1, 2016 – May 31, 2018

#### Qualifying Major and CIP Code
Found in the program of study section on page one of the new format I-20 (or primary major on the top of page three of the old format I-20).

#### Based on Prior Degree?
- If currently on OPT using a STEM-eligible degree: select “No”.
- If currently on OPT using a non-STEM-eligible degree and applying using a prior STEM-eligible degree: select “Yes”.

#### Employment Authorization Number
Equivalent to the USCIS # shown on the front of the EAD card.

---

### Section 3: Employer Information

#### NAICS Code
If employer/HR cannot provide it, refer to the full listing of NAICS codes provided by the U.S. Census Bureau.

#### Start Date of Employment
This is the start date of STEM OPT training with the employer and may differ from the actual start date with the employer. This date should match the start date listed under the STEM OPT requested period in Section 1.

---

### Section 5: Training Plan for STEM OPT Students

#### Site Name
Employer’s site name (may match employer name in Section 3). If working at a branch/subsidiary or other location, provide the work site name and physical location (not a website).

#### Name of Official
The employer official listed here will also be listed as the supervisor in the SEVIS record.

---

### Evaluations on Student Progress (Important)
Do not complete the Evaluation of Student Progress and Final Evaluation of Student Progress (pages six–seven) at initial submission. These evaluations should be submitted after 12 and 24 months of STEM OPT, respectively.

---

### Signatures
Student and employer official signatures are required in Sections 2, 4, and 6, as well as the evaluations. Handwritten ink signatures or official electronic signatures are accepted.
