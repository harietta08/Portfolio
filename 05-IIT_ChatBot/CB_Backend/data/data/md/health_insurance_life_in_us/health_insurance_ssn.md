## Clean Policy Text

### What is a Social Security Number (SSN)?
A Social Security Number (SSN) is issued to track earnings over a worker’s lifetime and can be used for life. The SSN is not a work permit. If you intend to work in the U.S., you must first obtain work authorization and then apply for an SSN.

### Who is Eligible to Apply for an SSN?
If you do not work on campus but receive a taxable scholarship, apply for an Individual Tax Identification Number (ITIN) for tax purposes.

To get an SSN, you must show SSA that you:
- Are eligible to work in the U.S.
- Are a full-time F-1/J-1 student at Illinois Tech
- Have one of:
  - On-campus job offer, OR
  - CPT authorization, OR
  - OPT authorization, OR
  - Academic Training authorization

An SSN is not required for driver’s license, cell phone, credit card, insurance, admission to an academic institution, or other non-work reasons. Dependents in F-2 status are not eligible for an SSN.

## SSN Application Workflows

### F-1 Students with On-Campus Employment

#### Step 1: Obtain an on-campus position
Positions are posted through Handshake.

#### Step 2: Obtain an on-campus job offer letter
Hiring department coordinator initiates the request with the Student Employment Office (SEO). Student receives the letter by email and must obtain signatures from the hiring department/supervisor and OGS.

#### Step 3: Obtain signed SSN support letter from OGS
Submit a request via the ISSS Portal:
- Log into ISSS Portal
- Upload the SEO SSN letter in “Letter Request”
- OGS emails when the signed letter is ready for download

#### Step 4: Apply for SSN
Call SSA to schedule an appointment. Complete and submit the SSN application. Take signed SSN letter and verification documents to SSA on appointment date.

Note: Newly arrived students may need to wait a few days after arriving so the I-94 entry appears in SSA systems.

#### Step 5: Visit SSA office (document presentation requirement)
You must visit the SSA office and show documents within **45 calendar days** of completing the online application.

Bring:
- Original on-campus employment letter
- Original SSN support letter from OGS
- Form I-20
- Valid passport (and old passport if it contains the F-1 visa)
- I-94 record
- Confirmation that SS-5 online was completed

After SSA appointment, student receives a receipt indicating the card will be mailed within 2–4 weeks. Bring the receipt and hiring paperwork to SEO for processing. Students may submit employee documents plus SSN receipt while waiting. After receiving the SSN card, student must bring it to SEO to update records.

---

### F-1 Students Authorized for CPT
If applying for SSN because first job is CPT:
- Complete SSN application online
- Visit local SSA office with documents

Apply no earlier than **30 days before** scheduled employment start date.

Bring:
- CPT I-20 issued by OGS
- Passport
- I-94 record
- Confirmation of SS-5 online completion

---

### F-1 Students Authorized for OPT
If SSN was not requested in OPT application:
- Complete SSN application online
- Visit local SSA office with documents

Apply **on or after** the start date on the EAD card.

Bring:
- OPT I-20 issued by OGS
- Passport
- EAD card
- I-94 record
- Confirmation of SS-5 online completion

---

### J-1 Students Authorized for Academic Training
- Obtain employment authorization documentation from OGS adviser or sponsoring agency
- Complete SSN application online
- Visit local SSA office with documents

Bring:
- DS-2019 with Academic Training notation
- Passport
- I-94 record
- Confirmation of SS-5 online completion

Dependents: J-2 dependents with employment authorization must also bring their EAD card.

---

### Faculty, Staff, and Scholars
Eligibility notes:
- J-1 scholars receiving salary from Illinois Tech must complete OGS check-in and wait **3–4 business days** before applying.
- Must be receiving funds from the university or another U.S. entity.

Application materials:
- Completed Form SS-5 (available at SSA office or socialsecurity.gov)
- Passport
- Visa
- I-94 admission number
- DS-2019

Submit application at local SSA office with supporting documents.


## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to get an SSN?
After OGS receives a complete request, a signed SSN letter is available for download within **2 business days**. After applying with SSA, it may take **4+ weeks** for SSA to mail the card.

### Do I need an SSN to begin working?
With authorization (on-campus/CPT/OPT) and a pending SSN application, students can start working without the SSN.

### My SSN card says “not valid for employment.” Can I still use it?
SSN card is used for tax purposes.

### Do I need an SSN to apply for a job?
No.

### What if my SSN card is lost or stolen?
If you do not currently have an on-campus job or off-campus work authorization, SSA will not issue a replacement card. Follow the stated steps to request a replacement card (link not provided in pasted text).
