## Clean Policy Text

### Responsibility to Maintain Status
Maintaining status as an F-1 or J-1 student is the sole responsibility of the student. The U.S. Department of Homeland Security (F-1) and the U.S. Department of State (J-1) do not consider ignorance of the law a legitimate reason for failing to obey it. The information provided is intended to support awareness of and compliance with the rules regulating F-1/J-1 status.

### Full-Time Enrollment Requirements (Fall & Spring)
All international students must meet full-time enrollment requirements:
- All undergraduate and Kent-Law students must be enrolled/registered for a minimum of **12 credit hours** each semester.
- All graduate students must be enrolled/registered for a minimum of **9 credit hours** each semester.
- Graduate students with a teaching or research assistantship may register for a minimum of **6 credit hours** to maintain status.

### Online Courses
All F-1 and J-1 students are permitted to take **only one online class** counting toward full-time eligibility each semester.

### Reduced Course Load
If a student is registering for less than full-time and meets the requirements for a Reduced Course Load, the student must submit a request within the ISSS Portal.

### Add/Drop Deadline
There is a deadline each semester for international students to add or drop a class, and the date is available on the Registrar’s Office website. It is important to be fully enrolled by this date. Failure to be enrolled full-time by the add/drop deadline may negatively impact immigration status.

### Enrollment Reporting
The Office of Global Services is required to report enrollment information to the Department of Homeland Security each semester; therefore, students must enroll appropriately.
