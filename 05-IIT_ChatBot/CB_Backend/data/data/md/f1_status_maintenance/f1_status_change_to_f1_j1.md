# Title: Changing to F-1 or J-1 Status (Change of Status vs Travel for Visa)

## Key Points (5–10 bullets)
- Issuance of an I-20/DS-2019 does **not** give a student F-1/J-1 status inside the U.S.
- There are two paths to obtain F-1/J-1 status: (1) change of status inside the U.S. or (2) travel abroad to apply for a visa and re-enter.
- Change of status eligibility requires valid non-immigrant status and no status violations/overstay.
- Certain statuses are not eligible for change of status (C, D, K, M, WT/WB; and some J-1/J-2 with 2-year home residency requirement).
- Some statuses do not allow study until the change of status is approved (example: B-1/B-2).
- Students cannot work on-campus or receive off-campus work authorization until F-1/J-1 status is approved and enrollment requirements are met.
- For Option 1, students file Form I-539 with supporting documents and fee requirements.
- For Option 2, students apply for a visa stamp at a U.S. embassy/consulate and then re-enter in F-1/J-1 status.
- Students cannot enter the U.S. in F-1/J-1 status until within **30 days** of the I-20/DS-2019 start date.

## Rules / Requirements
- I-20/DS-2019 issuance alone does not grant F-1/J-1 status in the U.S.
- Two ways to obtain F-1/J-1 status:
  - Option 1: Apply for a change of status within the U.S.
  - Option 2: Apply for an F-1/J-1 visa abroad and re-enter
- Eligibility to change status within the U.S. includes:
  - Valid non-immigrant status
  - Admission to Illinois Tech
  - Illinois Tech-issued I-20/DS-2019
  - Not in C, D, K, or M status
  - Not WT/WB (Visa Waiver Program)

  - No overstay or status violation
- Status maintenance requirement:
  - Maintain current status until at least 30 days before the I-20/DS-2019 start date and have USCIS receipt notice for the application
- Study and employment limitations:
  - Some statuses do not permit study until approval (example: B-1/B-2)
  - F-2 may study part-time until change to F-1 is approved
  - No on-campus work until F-1/J-1 status is approved
  - No off-campus employment authorization until approval and enrollment requirements are met

## Clean Policy Text

### Overview
Issuance of an I-20/DS-2019 does not give a student F-1/J-1 status in the United States. There are two ways to obtain the new status:
- Option 1: Applying for change of status to F-1/J-1 from within the U.S.
- Option 2: Applying for an F-1/J-1 visa at a U.S. embassy/consulate abroad

### Eligibility to Change Status Within the United States
- Valid non-immigrant status
- Admittance to Illinois Institute of Technology
- Illinois Tech-issued I-20/DS-2019
- Not be in C, D, K, or M status
- Not be a visitor with WT or WB status (Visa Waiver Program)
- Not be a J-1 or J-2 non-immigrant with a two-year home residency requirement
- Have not overstayed or violated status

### Important Points
Students must continue to maintain their current status until at least 30 days before the start date of the I-20/DS-2019 and must have a USCIS receipt notice confirming the application was received.

Some statuses do not permit study, including B-1/B-2. Individuals in such statuses are not permitted to enroll in or attend courses until the change of status has been approved.

Individuals in F-2 status may study part-time until their change of status application to F-1 is approved.

Students may not work on-campus until F-1/J-1 status has been approved. This includes teaching and research assistant positions. J-1 students must submit and be approved for on-campus employment if they will be employed at Illinois Tech.

Students will not be eligible for off-campus employment authorization until F-1/J-1 status has been approved and enrollment requirements are met.

If assistance is needed when applying for change of status, students may refer to a list of immigration attorneys.

### Option 1 — Apply for a Change of Status within the U.S.

#### Obtain an I-20/DS-2019 from Illinois Tech
Email **globalservices@illinoistech.edu** with:
- Valid copy of the biographical page of passport
- Recent financial document

#### Pay the SEVIS Fee
Pay the SEVIS fee to the U.S. Department of Homeland Security and retain the receipt. The I-20/DS-2019 and SEVIS ID number are needed to pay the fee. The SEVIS receipt must be submitted with the change of status application to USCIS.

#### Prepare Application Materials
- Illinois Tech-issued I-20/DS-2019 (signed on page 1)
- SEVIS fee receipt
- Completed Form I-539 (use USCIS website for latest version and instructions)
- Note: Beginning Oct. 1, 2023, USCIS exempts the $85 biometric services fee for Form I-539
- Completed Form G-1145 (download from USCIS website)
- Processing fee (check/money order to U.S. Department of Homeland Security; follow I-539 instructions)
- Letter of acceptance from Illinois Tech
- Original financial documents showing funds for tuition and living expenses  
  - Note: J-1 applications must show funding for the full DS-2019 duration
- Cover letter explaining circumstances for change of status
- Photocopy of passport (biographic pages, visa stamp, expiration date)
- Original I-94 card or electronic printout
- If holding dependent status (F-2/J-2/H-4, etc.), include copies of the principal status documents (I-94, visa, passport ID page, DS-2019/I-20, I-797, etc.)

#### File the Application
- File the application online, OR
- Mail the application to USCIS (see direct filing addresses)

#### After Filing
Mail applications to the address listed on the Form I-539 direct filing addresses and keep copies of all pages submitted.

Students receive a USCIS receipt in two to four weeks. Processing times vary; students should refer to USCIS processing times and may track the case on the USCIS website.

If approved, email OGS at **global@illinoistech.edu** with the USCIS approval notice. OGS confirms the first semester that the I-20 is fully under F-1/J-1 status.

Students should carefully review OGS guidance, including enrollment requirements, because maintaining legal status is the student’s responsibility.

Email is the primary form of communication; messages are sent to the university email address. Failure to monitor email may result in loss of immigration status.

If denied, USCIS notifies the student. If the underlying status is still valid, the student may remain in that status. If underlying status has expired, the student must prepare to depart and meet with an OGS adviser to discuss options.

### Option 2 — Apply for a Visa by Traveling Abroad

#### Obtain an I-20/DS-2019 from Illinois Tech
Email **global@illinoistech.edu** with:
- Valid copy of the biographical page of passport
- Recent financial document

#### Pay the SEVIS Fee
Pay the SEVIS fee and keep the receipt. The I-20/DS-2019 and SEVIS ID are required. Information about paying the fee is available online.

#### Apply for a Visa Stamp
Apply for the corresponding visa stamp at a U.S. embassy/consulate. It is recommended to apply in the home country. For third-country applications, consult the embassy/consulate for procedures and guidelines.

#### Re-enter and Check In
Upon re-entry in F-1 or J-1 status, the student must immediately check in with OGS and bring:
- I-20 or DS-2019
- Passport with visa stamp
- I-94 printout

Students are not permitted to enter until it is within **30 days** of the start date on the I-20/DS-2019.
