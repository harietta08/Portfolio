## Key Points (5–10 bullets)
- For travel within the U.S., students should carry core immigration documents (passport, I-94, I-20/DS-2019, and/or I-797 copy).
- For travel abroad, re-entry requires the right combination of: valid passport, valid visa (if required), properly signed I-20/DS-2019, I-94, and proof of enrollment/employment.
- Travel signature validity differs by status (continuing student vs OPT/AT; J-1 scholar types).
- A Reprint I-20/DS-2019 request through the ISSS Portal is used to obtain an updated travel signature.
- “Electronic” I-20/DS-2019 issuance is supported; agencies accept a printed copy; students must print/sign as required.
- At the port of entry, students must hand-carry documents (do not check them) and should verify their admission stamp/I-94 accuracy.
- If documents are missing/incorrect, a traveler may receive Form I-515A (limited entry) and must contact OGS promptly.
- Automatic Visa Revalidation (AVR) may allow re-entry without a valid visa for certain short trips to Canada/Mexico/adjacent islands, with major limitations and exclusions.

## Rules / Requirements
- Travel within the U.S.: carry valid passport, I-94 (card/printout), I-20/DS-2019, and/or copy of I-797 approval (as applicable).
- Puerto Rico / U.S. Virgin Islands / Northern Mariana Islands: visa not required unless traveling to other countries during the trip; immigration documents must still be carried.
- Re-entering the U.S. (F-1/J-1 students and dependents):
  - I-20/DS-2019 with valid travel signature:
    - signed within last 12 months (continuing student)
    - signed within last 6 months (if on OPT/AT)
  - Valid visa stamp (if required)
  - Passport valid at least 6 months beyond return date
  - Evidence of financial support
  - SEVIS (I-901) fee receipt
  - Proof of current enrollment or proof of employment if on OPT/AT
- Additional OPT/AT re-entry documents:
  - EAD card (or Academic Training DS-2019)
  - Offer letter or proof of employment (pay stubs/employment verification)
- Re-entering the U.S. (J-1 scholars and dependents):
  - DS-2019 travel signature validity:
    - last 6 months (short-term scholar)
    - last 12 months (research scholar/professor)
  - Valid visa stamp (if required)
  - Passport valid at least 6 months beyond return date
  - Host department letter confirming activity
- Re-entering the U.S. (H-1B employees and dependents):
  - Valid passport
  - Original I-797 approval notice
  - I-94 issued for H-1B
  - Recent paystub
  - Current employment letter
  - Copy of I-129 and LCA from OGS
- Port of entry handling:
  - Do not place immigration documents in checked baggage.
  - New I-94 number is typically issued each entry (unless using AVR).
  - Verify admission stamp status and D/S (F-1/J-1) or I-797 end date (H-1B) before leaving POE.
- I-515A risk:
  - If documents are insufficient, traveler may receive Form I-515A (limited entry) and must contact OGS during walk-in hours or front desk.
- Automatic Visa Revalidation (AVR):
  - F-1/J-1: may re-enter without a valid visa after travel to Canada/Mexico/adjacent islands for < 30 days (no additional countries), if visa not canceled.
  - H-1B/H-4: AVR applies only to Canada/Mexico for < 30 days; valid visa required from adjacent islands.
  - AVR requires a valid (unexpired) POE admission stamp or paper I-94 endorsed by DHS.
  - AVR not available for citizens of Iran, Syria, Sudan, or Cuba (subject to change).
  - AVR not valid if traveling from Canada/Mexico to another country and then returning via Canada/Mexico.
  - AVR not available if applying for a new U.S. visa; if denied or pending, re-entry rules change (must wait for issuance; denial requires return to home country to obtain a new visa).

## Clean Policy Text

### Travel Within the United States
For travel within the United States, carry immigration documents in a safe place: valid passport, I-94 card/printout, I-20/DS-2019, and/or copy of I-797 approval (as applicable).

If traveling to Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands, or the Northern Mariana Islands, a valid visa is not required unless traveling to other countries while going to or coming from those locations. Immigration documents should still be carried.

### Traveling Abroad — Students (F-1/J-1)
Students and dependents need documents to re-enter and resume studies or OPT/Academic Training:
- I-20/DS-2019 with valid travel signature:
  - signed within last 12 months if continuing student
  - signed within last 6 months if on OPT/AT
- Valid visa stamp
- Passport valid at least six months after return date
- Evidence of financial support
- SEVIS (I-901) fee receipt
- Proof of current enrollment or proof of OPT/AT employment

Additional OPT/AT documents:
- EAD card or Academic Training DS-2019 (EAD card alone does not guarantee re-entry)
- Offer letter or proof of employment (recent pay stubs or employment verification letter)

### Traveling Abroad — J-1 Scholars
J-1 scholars and dependents need:
- DS-2019 with valid travel signature:
  - last six months (short-term scholar)
  - last 12 months (research scholar or professor)
- Valid visa stamp
- Passport valid at least six months after return date
- Host department letter confirming activity

### Traveling Abroad — H-1B Employees
H-1B employees and dependents need:
- Valid passport
- Original I-797 approval notice
- I-94 card issued for H-1B
- Recent paystub
- Current employment letter
- Copy of I-129 and Labor Condition Application from OGS

### How To Obtain a Travel Signature
Submit a Reprint I-20/DS-2019 request through the ISSS Portal. The request is processed within 7 business days, and the I-20/DS-2019 is available for download in the portal.

Electronic I-20/DS-2019 issuance is authorized indefinitely; agencies accept printed copies. Illinois Tech does not mail/ship original forms; students download, print, and use the printed copy as needed.

### Traveling to Canada, Mexico, and Adjacent Islands
Canada, Mexico, and adjacent islands have their own entry visa rules. Travelers may need a visa to enter those countries/territories.

Adjacent islands include Saint Pierre; Miquelon; Dominican Republic; Haiti; Bermuda; Bahamas; Barbados; Jamaica; Windward and Leeward Islands; Trinidad; Martinique; and other British/French/Netherlands Caribbean territories or possessions, except Cuba.

Note: For H-1B/H-4, AVR applies only to Canada and Mexico. A valid visa is required to return from adjacent islands.

### Re-Entering the United States — Port of Entry (POE)
Carry all immigration documents and do not check them in luggage.

With each new entry, travelers typically receive a new I-94 number (unless using AVR). The POE officer stamps the passport and returns documents. Travelers must verify the stamp is legible and accurate (status and D/S for F-1/J-1, or end date of I-797 for H-1B). Print I-94 records for traveler and dependents; dependent stamps should reflect the appropriate dependent status (F-2/J-2/H-4). If an I-94 is lost, it must be reprinted.

If proper documents are not available, a traveler may receive Form I-515A for limited entry and must contact OGS for assistance.

### How To Avoid Getting Form I-515A
Hand-carry (do not check) the following:
- Valid passport
- Signed Form I-20 (F/M) or signed Form DS-2019 (J) for traveler/dependents
- Evidence of financial support (as applicable)
- Evidence of acceptance/enrollment (e.g., tuition receipt or acceptance letter)
- Paper receipt for SEVIS I-901 fee
- Name/contact info for the DSO (including a 24-hour emergency contact number)

### Automatic Visa Revalidation (AVR)

#### F-1/J-1
A valid U.S. entry visa may not be required to re-enter the U.S. if traveling to Canada, Mexico, or adjacent islands for less than 30 days without traveling to additional countries, and the visa has not been canceled.

#### H-1B/H-4
AVR applies only to travel to Canada and Mexico for less than 30 days. A valid visa is required to return from adjacent islands.

#### AVR Requirements and Limitations
- Requires a valid (unexpired) POE admission stamp or paper I-94 endorsed by DHS.
- Not available for citizens of Iran, Syria, Sudan, or Cuba (subject to change).
- Not valid if traveling from Canada/Mexico to another country and returning via Canada/Mexico.
- Not available if applying for a new U.S. visa; if a visa application is pending, the traveler cannot re-enter while pending; if denied, return to home country is required to obtain a new visa.

### Obtaining or Renewing a Visa
Visa document requirements vary by country; travelers should consult the U.S. consulate where applying. Plan ahead due to possible security checks that can add 30 days or more. Evidence of financial resources must typically be dated within the last six months.

If applying for a visa outside the home country, approval is not guaranteed. If difficulties occur, contact OGS and be prepared with visa processing information and consulate-provided details.

### Obtaining or Renewing an F-1/J-1 Visa in Canada or Mexico
Before applying in Canada/Mexico, determine whether the embassy/consulate accepts Third Country Nationals and whether a visa to enter Canada/Mexico is required. Visa wait times should be checked.

If denied a visa in Canada/Mexico, re-entry under AVR is not permitted and travelers may be required to return home to reapply. Prior immigration status violations may affect eligibility to apply outside the home country; travelers should consult OGS.

## Administrative Deadlines / Time Windows (Snapshot – verify official page)
- Travel signature validity:
  - Continuing student: signed within last **12 months**
  - OPT/AT: signed within last **6 months**
  - J-1 short-term scholar: signed within last **6 months**
  - J-1 research scholar/professor: signed within last **12 months**
- Reprint I-20/DS-2019 processing time: **7 business days**
- AVR time window: travel duration **< 30 days**
- Visa security checks may add **30+ days** to processing time (estimate)
