- Receiving an I-20/DS-2019 does **not** guarantee a visa; it shows eligibility to apply based on documented funds and academic record.
- Students cannot begin the visa application process until they receive the proper immigration form.
- Illinois Tech creates the I-20/DS-2019 after admission acceptance, deposit payment, and submission of passport and financials.
- After issuance, students must print, sign, pay the SEVIS I-901 fee, and apply for the visa.
- Entry to the U.S. is allowed up to **30 days before** the program start date.
- Check-in at Illinois Tech is required after arrival for Initial Attendance and Transfer Pending cases.
- Illinois Tech issues electronic I-20s/DS-2019s; students must print and sign the form.
- I-20/DS-2019 are used for travel, identification, DMV/SSA, and employment verification.
- Program length on the form is based on degree level and may require extension if more time is needed.

## Clean Policy Text

### What is an I-20 or DS-2019?
Form I-20s are “Certificates of Eligibility” for the F (student) visa status. Form DS-2019s are “Certificates of Eligibility” for the J (exchange) visa status. The form is not a guarantee that a student will receive a visa; instead, it shows the U.S. government that the student is considered eligible to apply for an F-1 Student Visa (I-20) or J-1 Exchange Visa (DS-2019) based on documented funds and academic record.

Students cannot begin the visa application process until they have received the proper immigration form.

### Step 1: Requirements To Receive Form I-20 or DS-2019
To create the Form I-20 or DS-2019, Illinois Tech requires students to:
- Receive an official admission/acceptance from Illinois Institute of Technology
- Pay the non-refundable admission deposit fee
- Submit a valid passport and acceptable financials within the Applicant Status Page

Once the final step is completed, an OGS Application Processor reviews the request and creates the immigration form within **5 business days**. The student receives an email from **global@illinoistech.edu** with the form as an attachment and next steps.

### Step 2: After Receiving Form I-20 or DS-2019 (Initial Attendance)
If the “Form Issue Reason” on the I-20 is **Initial Attendance**, students should follow these general steps:
- Print the Form I-20 or DS-2019 and sign at the bottom of Page 1 (the form is provided as a PDF by email)
- Pay the SEVIS I-901 fee
- Apply for the F-1 Student Visa (I-20) or J-1 Exchange Visa (DS-2019)
- Review incoming international student information
- Prepare travel to the U.S. (arrival may be up to **30 days prior** to the listed program start date)
- After arriving in the U.S., complete the International Student Check-In process at Illinois Tech

### Special Cases by “Form Issue Reason”
- **Initial Attendance – Change of Status Requested:** follow the general next steps to apply for a Change of Status within the U.S. (Option 1).
- **Transfer Pending:** as a current F-1 student, you may travel internationally with a valid visa and the transfer I-20; complete Check-In at Illinois Tech within 30 days of the intended semester start.
- **Change of Education:** continuing at the same U.S. university; Check-In is not completed again.

### FAQs (Policy Content)

#### How is the Form I-20 or DS-2019 created?
After admission, deposit payment, and required immigration documents are submitted, OGS staff enter information into the U.S. government database **SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System)**. SEVIS processes the information and generates the Form I-20 (F-1) or DS-2019 (J-1) as a PDF.

Only a **DSO** can generate and update an I-20, and only an **ARO** can generate and update a DS-2019. Office of Global Services staff members are DSOs and AROs. Errors or questions should be directed to **global@illinoistech.edu**.

#### Do all international students need a Form I-20 or DS-2019?
No. If a student is maintaining a U.S. legal status that allows study and is not F-1 or J-1, then an I-20 or DS-2019 is not needed. I-20 is required for students who wish to be in F-1 status; DS-2019 is required for students who wish to be in J-1 status.

#### What is the difference between an Electronic or Original Form?
Illinois Tech issues electronic I-20s and DS-2019s. Agencies accept a printed copy. Students must download, print, and sign their I-20 or DS-2019.

#### How is the Form I-20 or DS-2019 used outside of the U.S.?
Students use the form to apply for an F-1 or J-1 visa at a U.S. embassy/consulate. Students must present both the visa and the I-20/DS-2019 to a U.S. Customs and Border Protection officer at the port of entry.

#### How is the Form I-20 or DS-2019 used inside of the U.S.?
The form is used as identification and proof of legal and academic status. It is commonly required at the Social Security Office and DMV, and may be used in employment onboarding. Off-campus employment authorization is printed on page 2 of the I-20 or page 1 of the DS-2019.

#### How is the length of time determined on the Form I-20 or DS-2019?
Program length is based on the student’s intended degree and educational level and is an estimate for completing degree requirements:
- Bachelor’s Degree: 5 years
- Master / MS / MBA: 2 years
- PhD: 6 years
- Visiting/Exchange: varies (typically one or two semesters)

If a student completes earlier than the program end date, the form may be shortened to reflect degree conferral date. If a student cannot finish by the program end date, a program extension request must be submitted in the ISSS Portal prior to expiration.

#### Why is the major name on the form different from the Illinois Tech program name?
SEVIS requires standardized program identifiers called **CIP codes**. The program name on the form reflects the CIP code name, which may differ from the university’s degree title.

#### Should students keep copies of old I-20s or DS-2019s?
Yes. Students should keep copies of previous forms for records, as government agencies like USCIS may request them. Typically, the most recent form is used for travel and legal purposes.

#### Why do students have multiple forms, and which one should be used?
New I-20s or DS-2019s are issued when there are significant updates to the SEVIS record (biographical data, program dates, change of major, employment authorization, etc.). The most recent updated form should be used for travel and U.S. government matters.
