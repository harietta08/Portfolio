## Key Points (5–10 bullets)
- Students in F-1/J-1 status must get approval from the Office of Global Services (OGS) and their academic department/adviser before dropping below full-time.
- Reduced Course Load (RCL) is allowed only for specific U.S. government–approved reasons.
- Requests must be submitted in the ISSS Portal as **“F-1 Reduced Course Load”** or **“J-1 Reduced Course Load.”**
- The request requires academic adviser electronic confirmation through the portal.
- Reasons 1–2 are due by the **add/drop deadline**.
- For Reasons 3–4, students must consult an international student adviser before dropping/withdrawing.
- Unacceptable reasons (e.g., financial hardship, probation) do not qualify for RCL.
- Medical RCL is limited to a cumulative **12 months per academic program**.

## Rules / Requirements
- **Pre-approval required:** OGS + academic department/adviser approval is required before taking a reduced course load.
- **Submission required:** File an “F-1 Reduced Course Load” or “J-1 Reduced Course Load” request in the ISSS Portal.
- **Adviser confirmation required:** Academic adviser must complete an electronic confirmation as part of the request.
- **Deadlines:**
  - Reasons **1–2**: request due by **add/drop deadline**.
  - Reasons **3–4**: must consult an international student adviser before dropping/withdrawing.
- **Unacceptable reasons (not allowed):**
  - Impending failure in a course
  - Academic probation
  - Financial hardship
  - Intent to delay graduation
- **Acceptable reasons (allowed):**
  1) Completion of Program  
  2) Graduate Students Taking Qualifying/Comprehensive Exam  
  3) Academic Difficulties (one-time use; minimum 6 credits)  
  4) Medical Condition (maximum 12 months total per academic program)

## Clean Policy Text

### Overview
Students must have approval from the Office of Global Services and the academic department/academic adviser before taking a reduced course load. Only a limited set of U.S. government–approved reasons allow students in F-1/J-1 status to be enrolled less than full-time.

### How to Apply
To apply for a reduced course load, submit an **“F-1 Reduced Course Load”** or **“J-1 Reduced Course Load”** request in the ISSS Portal. As part of the request, the student provides the Academic Advisor’s details so the adviser can complete an electronic confirmation.

### Timing and Advising Requirements
- For **Reasons 1–2**, the request is due by the **add/drop deadline**.
- For **Reasons 3–4**, the student must consult with an international student adviser before dropping or withdrawing from courses.
Failure to follow instructions may result in a **violation of status**.

### Unacceptable Reasons to Drop Below Full-Time
- Impending failure in a course
- Academic probation
- Financial hardship
- Intent to delay graduation

### Acceptable Reasons to Drop Below Full-Time

#### 1) Completion of Program
Students do not need to be enrolled full-time during the term they are graduating. A completed Reduced Course Load form signed by the academic adviser must be submitted by the **first day of the semester** in which the student is graduating.

**Notes**
- The last and only course cannot be an online course.
- Co-op and internship enrollment are considered live courses.
- The student will receive a new DS-2019 or I-20 with a new end date reflecting the end of the term.

#### 2) Graduate Students Taking Qualifying/Comprehensive Exam
If a graduate student is taking the qualifying/comprehensive examination during the semester and will only be registered for **six credits**, this may be used **only one time**. Students must enroll full-time during all subsequent semesters of coursework, even if exams need to be repeated.

#### 3) Academic Difficulties
This reason may be used **only one time**. Students must be enrolled for a minimum of **six credits**. The following circumstances apply:
- Difficulties with English language or reading requirements in the first term of study in the United States
- Unfamiliarity with American teaching methods in the first term of study in the U.S.
- Improper course level placement (dropping allowed only when the adviser recommended a course for which the student was not academically prepared)

#### 4) Medical Condition
Students must show proof from a licensed medical doctor, psychiatrist, doctor of osteopathy, licensed psychologist, or clinical psychologist that they should take a reduced course load or refrain from taking courses during the current term. Students cannot accumulate more than **12 months** of Reduced Course Load exemption for this reason per academic program.
